Shared space, band width, informational landscape

How can shared digital space and shared physical space intersect?

Purely digital, purely physical and hybrid spaces

The social affordances of shared physical spaces - offices, corridors, cafes, parks, squares - are often studied.

Social behaviour in purely digital space is also well studied, for example through 'big data' the ways memes go viral across the web.

However, Many communities have both digital and physical facets. For example, a geographical area has cafes and squares, but much useful information will also exist on blogs and forums.

Are there design interventions that can open a portal between digital and physical space?!

Social norms and the ergonomics of attention are key differences between shared physical space and shared digital space.

Pure physical space

  • Peripheral vision gives everyone a sense of what everyone else is doing
  • Social norms require a high degree of social interaction

Pure digital space

  • Many open source project successfully coordinate an build community through only digital share space
  • When a project is purely digital, by definition only those who are prepared to regularly engage in digital space will participate
  • Developers are weird

Hybrid space

  • In hybrid space there are weak social norms: often checking email is the only strongly enforced activity in shared digital space.
  • In hybrid space, there is no peripheral vision: unless you specifically choose to, you will not 'bump into' each other in digital space.
  • The connection between digital space and physical space is very weak, for example a screen typically takes up 0.5% of the visual field

In summary...

Two people sharing a physical space have a small information space, plenty of bandwidth to do so with, and strong social norms to bind each other.

Two people in digital space have a virtually infinite information space to explore, with tiny bandwidth to use and weak social conventions

...so a good design intervention would...

  • Increase bandwidth between individuals and shared digital space
  • Increase the desire to participate
  • Be peripherally visible
