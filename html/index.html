BBC Lab UK

Sometimes, scientists need big samples to deliver valid research. We worked to make scientific experiments fun enough that 100,000 people will participate via a website while generating genuinely new science.

I worked on the Lab UK projects as a researcher. During my time we had over 100,000 participants in our experiments, leading to a scientific paper published in Nature.

RIFFF

Music is always affected by medium - from Beethoven writing for the newly-invented iron-frame piano to the length of a side of vinyl giving us the three-minute pop song. Rifff asks: what does listening on your iPhone mean for music?

Rifff is a project to explore the computational power inheriant in modern playback devices - mobile phones and laptops.

I've done all the design and development on the project, working iteratively with musician Tom Bryan. You can see the code here, and the project here.
