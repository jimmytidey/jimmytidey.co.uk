Random Seed

 

Concept

Once, owning a piece of vinyl was the only way to have a copy of a song. In that sense, the vinyl was the single or album. Now, there are two separate things. Music no longer has any intrinsic physical form. It could be on YouTube, on a CD, or just an MP3 file.

The question that started the Random Seed project was: How could a composition be inherently physical?

It's a tough problem, because no matter what you do to protect a piece of music, someone can record it and upload it to YouTube.

Unless. Unless it's different each time. A device that plays back a song which is (slightly) different each time can't be uploaded anywhere. That's what Random Seed intends to be.

An interesting consequence of this is that Random Seed takes full advantage of what the web can do - go beyond linear playback.
